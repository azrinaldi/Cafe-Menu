Sutan Sjahrir

The Smiling Diplomat

Here's a time line of Sutan Sjahrir's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Padang Panjang, West Sumatra, Dutch East Indies.
  • 1926 - Moves to the Netherlands to pursue a law degree at Leiden University.
  • 1931 - Joins the Indonesian National Party (PNI) and actively participates in nationalist activities.
  • 1932 - Co-founds the Indonesian National Education Foundation (Yayasan Perguruan Nasional Indonesia) to advocate for education among Indonesians.
  • 1933 - Returns to the Dutch East Indies and establishes a successful career as a lawyer in Batavia (now Jakarta).
  • 1940 - Arrested by the Dutch colonial authorities due to his nationalist activities and imprisoned for two years.
  • 1942 - Released from prison following the Japanese occupation of the Dutch East Indies.
  • 1945 - Plays a crucial role in drafting the Indonesian Declaration of Independence and serves as the first prime minister of Indonesia.
  • 1946 - Resigns from the position of prime minister due to political differences and establishes the Socialist Party of Indonesia (Partai Sosialis Indonesia).
  • 1947 - Arrested by the Dutch colonial authorities during the Dutch-Indonesian conflict and forced into exile in the Netherlands.
  • 1949 - Returns to Indonesia after the recognition of Indonesian independence and becomes a prominent political figure.
  • 1955 - Leads the Indonesian delegation to the Bandung Conference, an influential gathering promoting solidarity among Asian and African nations.
  • 1957 - Resigns from the Socialist Party of Indonesia and establishes the People's Democratic Party (Partai Demokrasi Rakyat).
  • 1959 - Arrested and imprisoned by President Sukarno's government following political disagreements.
  • 1966 - Released from prison after the fall of Sukarno's regime and goes into self-exile in the Netherlands.
  • 1968 - Publishes his memoir, "Sjahrir: Politics and Exile."
